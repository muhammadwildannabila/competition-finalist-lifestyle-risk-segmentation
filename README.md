# 🧠 Project Overview

This project develops an explainable risk segmentation framework to identify individuals with elevated lifestyle-related health risks and improve the targeting accuracy of public health campaigns.

Rather than performing medical diagnosis, the system quantifies risk scores, segments the population into **Low Risk**, **Medium Risk**, and **High Risk** tiers, and generates actionable recommendations for preventive intervention.

The solution was developed as part of a team that reached the **Finalist** stage in the **Data Analyst Competition at DevFest Surabaya 2025**.

---

# 🎯 Project Objectives

- Quantify lifestyle-related health risks using behavioral and physiological indicators.
- Segment individuals into interpretable risk categories.
- Identify dominant risk drivers such as BMI, blood pressure, and physical inactivity.
- Generate targeted recommendations for public health campaigns.
- Support evidence-based public health decision-making.

# 🔍 Key Findings

- Approximately **28% of the population** was classified as **High Risk**.
- Blood pressure, BMI, and physical inactivity were the most influential risk drivers.
- Risk levels increased consistently with age and obesity indicators.
- Explainable segmentation supports transparent and actionable decision-making.
